History

- The "dandy horse," invented by Baron Karl von Drais in 1817, is considered the first bicycle but lacked pedals.

- The first mechanically propelled bicycle was likely created by Kirkpatrick MacMillan in 1839.

- In the 1860s, French inventors added pedals and a crank drive, leading to the mass-produced velocipede.

- The "penny-farthing" evolved from the velocipede but was difficult to ride due to its high seat and poor weight distribution.

- J.K. Starley's 1885 "safety bicycle" introduced a chain drive, lower seat, and improved weight distribution, paving the way for modern bicycles.

- Innovations like pneumatic tires (1888), freewheels, coaster brakes, and gears enhanced comfort and performance in the 1890s.

- Cycling clubs became popular in the 1870s, and companies like the Raleigh Bicycle Company rose to prominence.

- By the 20th century, bicycles became a global transport staple, with over 1 billion bicycles produced, and the Chinese Flying Pigeon became the most produced model.
